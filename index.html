How This Works

  1. Select a value for theta, alpa, max iterations, and inital weights
  2. Click "Submit"
  3. The graph will populate with a best fit line that divides the white and black
    • The graph is calculated using the values supplied for theta, alpha, max iterations, and inital weights

Items of note:

  • The two problems are simply different placement of the initial points
  • Max iterations is the maximum number of epochs to be run by the perceptron training algorithm. The system will stop at that number, regardless of any outstanding errors
  • Theta, alpha, and max iterations are plyable constants, which can be tweaked to train the perceptron faster.
    • These have been populated with possible values that make sense, given the nature of the perceptron training algorithm
  • You will get different best fit lines every time you click submit, as the perceptron will be re-trained from scratch
  • Random weights use values between -.5 and .5
